About Me

For the last thirteen years, I have been working to physically and culturally connect the world by designing, testing, and certifying airplanes at Boeing. In my last role at the Boeing Company, I served as the technical lead for the 777X airplane's Folding Wingtip System. My responsibilities included actuation and control of this new and novel system. I took the idea through the long, arduous process to be ready and safe for first flight. While introducing the first folding wing on a commercial airplane was fun, challenging, and rewarding, it took 7 years.

I chose to learn a new skill to challenge myself, feed my curiosity, and maintain a leading technology position in the job market. I am drawn to the field of software development because of its fast turn around and shorter schedules. I find creating and producing things enjoyable and fulfilling. I want to live the product lifecycle on a scale that is shorter than a decade. Not to mention, the ability software possesses to scale and affect so much of the world comes with a lot of power to improve the world.
